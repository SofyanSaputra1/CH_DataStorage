Impact of Adjuvant Chemotherapy Cycles on Prognosis of Resectable Stomach Cancer

Reward: CHC 2,000

Organization: Hong Kong Cancer Institute

Researcher Name(s): Wen-Ying Zhang, Wen-Jun Zhang, Yu Bai

Sample size: 1000

Purpose: The aim of this study is to investigate the effects of adjuvant chemotherapy cycles on the prognosis of patients with post-operative stomach cancer through retrospective analysis. No clear guidelines exist as to how many cycles of chemotherapy to administer following gastric tumor resection. Patients with gastric cancer who underwent gastrectomy, followed by adjuvant chemotherapy consisting of epirubicin, cisplatin or oxaliplatin, leucovorin, and 5- uorouracil, according to a de ned schedule, were divided into three groups according to the number of chemotherapy cycles: Group I (<6 cycles); Group II (6 cycles); and Group III (>6 cycles). Our primary endpoint is the 5 year relapse-free survival rate of patients.

Field Inclusion Criteria

Diagnosis Name: Gastric Cancer

Diagnosis Date: Over 5 years ago

Medication Taken: epirubicin, cisplatin, leucovorin, 5-Fluorouracil OR epirubicin, oxaloplatin, leucovorin, 5-Fluorouracil

Medication Taken Date: After date of first diagnosis

Medication Indication: Gastric Cancer
